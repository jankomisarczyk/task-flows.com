Who am I?

I thought that the best way to answer "Who am I?" is by sharing my sense of purpose. To me, that purpose is beautifully captured by the Japanese concept of Ikigai - a philosophy that's not just about your reason for living, but also about living a life full of joy and fulfillment. It helps you uncover the unique activities and dreams that truly matter to you, the ones that make you want to get out of bed each morning and work towards turning them into reality. Ikigai weaves together four elements:

  1. What I love

  2. What I am good at
    Numbers and I? We've always gotten along. I've never had trouble with math or physics; they were my favorite subjects in school and still bring me joy. Whether it's solving math puzzles or exploring the vast, beautiful mysteries of the universe, I love every bit of it.
  3. What I can be paid for
    By day, I work as a Software Developer, and by night, I'm a Quant. I enjoy both creating clean, elegant code and researching undervalued stocks (value and event-driven investing, if you're curious). These roles don't just pay the bills - they keep my curiosity alive and well-fed.
  4. What the world needs
    I believe the world needs more kindness, integrity, and people who stick to their moral compass. On a grander scale, I also believe we're standing at the edge of an exciting era with Artificial General Intelligence on the horizon - and I'm eager to be part of the team that helps bring it to life.
